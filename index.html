About the Event

The Ideathon 2025 The School of Engineering and Technology, Department of Information Technology, in association with TechTitans, proudly presents IDEATHON 2025 — a platform designed to ignite innovation, creativity, and problem-solving skills among students.

Students will submit their idea abstracts on 03.11.2025, which will be evaluated by the organizing committee. The shortlisted participants will be announced on 04.11.2025, and the final presentation will take place on 06.11.2025 at the 3rd Floor Lecture Hall, Academic Block.

Participants can choose from diverse problem domains such as Technology, Environment, Healthcare, Education, and Social Innovation, and propose creative, feasible, and impactful solutions.

Rules & Guidelines

To ensure a fair and exciting event for everyone, please adhere to the following rules:

  • Team Size: Each team can have a minimum of 1 and a maximum of 2 members.
  • Eligibility: All participants must be currently enrolled as students in any college or university.
  • Originality: All ideas and submissions must be the original work of the team. Plagiarism will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • Problem Statement: Teams must select one of the 20 provided problem statements as the foundation for their idea.
  • Abstract: A clear and concise abstract (max 500 words) describing your proposed solution must be submitted via the registration form.
  • Code of Conduct: All participants must treat each other with respect. Harassment or disruptive behavior will not be tolerated.
  • Submission: Students will submit their idea abstracts on 03.11.2025, which will be evaluated by the organizing committee. The shortlisted participants will be announced on 04.11.2025, and the final presentation will take place on 06.11.2025 at the 3rd Floor Lecture Hall, Academic Block.
  • Judging: The decision of the judging panel is final and binding. Criteria will include innovation, feasibility, impact, and presentation quality.

Problem Statements

Choose one of these challenges to focus your innovation.

PS-01: Technology

Smarter Digital Security Against Online Scams

Cyber fraud and digital scams are rising every year — how can people be protected from online financial threats using smarter digital security?

PS-02: Technology

Authenticity in Online Recruitment

Fake job listings and online recruitment scams are increasing — how can job seekers verify authenticity and stay protected online?

PS-03: Technology

Data Poisoning and AI Integrity

AI systems are being manipulated through poisoned data, reducing accuracy and trust — how can we ensure that machine learning models remain pure and tamper-proof?

PS-04: Technology

Offline Communication During Disasters

When disasters strike and the internet fails, communication collapses — how might people stay connected even without network coverage?

PS-05: Environment

Smarter Rainwater Harvesting

Rural communities often face water scarcity despite rainfall — how can we improve local rainwater harvesting and water distribution systems?

PS-06: Environment

Efficient Waste Segregation and Recycling

Improper waste segregation causes health hazards and environmental damage — how can we make waste sorting and recycling more efficient at the household level?

PS-07: Environment

Preventing Human–Wildlife Conflicts

Human–wildlife conflicts are rising as animals enter nearby farmlands — how can early detection and alerts prevent loss for both humans and wildlife?

PS-08: Environment

Cooling Cities Naturally

Urban rooftops absorb massive heat, worsening city temperatures — how could we redesign roofs or materials to cool our environment naturally?

PS-09: Environment

E-Waste Recycling Awareness

Millions of electronic devices end up as waste every year — how can citizens be encouraged and rewarded to recycle or donate their e-waste responsibly?

PS-10: Healthcare

Preventing Lifestyle Diseases

India faces an alarming rise in lifestyle diseases like obesity and diabetes — how can awareness and prevention be made part of daily routines?

PS-11: Healthcare

Early Stress Detection and Mental Wellness

Many people experience high stress levels without realizing it early — how can we detect stress and promote mental well-being in everyday life?

PS-12: Healthcare

Combating Counterfeit Medicines

Counterfeit medicines threaten patient safety and treatment outcomes — how might we verify medicine authenticity at the consumer level?

PS-13: Healthcare

Reducing Digital Eye Strain

Excessive screen time causes serious eye strain — how could technology help users track and prevent digital fatigue in real time?

PS-14: Education

Affordable Digital Education for Rural Schools

Students in rural schools lack digital access and interactive tools — how can we make digital education affordable and available everywhere?

PS-15: Education

Bridging Industry and Academia

College graduates often lack industry-ready skills — how can we build stronger collaboration between universities and industries?

PS-16: Education

Tackling Exam Stress and Anxiety

Many students struggle with exam stress and performance anxiety — how can mental health be better supported in education systems?

PS-17: Social Innovation

Safer Roads through Smart Awareness

Road accidents occur daily due to speeding and poor awareness — how can we build safer, tech-enabled traffic and driving systems?

PS-18: Social Innovation

Youth Employment and Skill Matching

Unemployment among youth remains a major concern — how can we create digital platforms that connect skill, talent, and opportunity effectively?

PS-19: Social Innovation

Combating Fake News During Disasters

Fake news spreads rapidly during disasters — how could technology detect and reduce misinformation to protect people in emergencies?

PS-20: Social Innovation

Community Energy Sharing Networks

Communities often waste excess solar power — how might neighborhoods share or trade renewable energy locally?
